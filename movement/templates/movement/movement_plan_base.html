    Welcome to Movement Planning page

    This module enables logistic planning officers to generate a variety of calculation outputs linked to road movement to assist them in the logistics planning process.

    Get Started

    Start Point

    A well-defined point on a route at which the movement of vehicles comes under the control of the commander of the movement. It is at this point that hte column is formed by the elements comprising it, passing in succession at their appointed times.

    Column

    A group of vehicles moving under a single commander over the same route, in the same direction, at the same time.

    Element

    Subdivision of a large column, each element having its own commander.

    Critical Point (CP)

    A selected point along a route used for reference in giving instructions.

    Release Point (RP)

    A well defined point on a route at which the elements comprising a column return to the authority of their respective commanders, each one of the elements continuing its movement towards its own appropriate destination.

    Halts

    A short halt by columns or elements of columns travelling on controlled routes takes place after two hours running and lasts 15 minutes. If necessary, a second halt takes place after a further two hours and lasts for 30 minutes. This pattern may be repeated up to a maximum of nine hours actual driving time, after which drivers should take a minimum of 11 hours unbroken rest before commencing driving again.

    Running Time

    The total time for which a single element is in transit.

    Pass Time

    The time that elapses between the moment when the leading vehicle of a column passes a given point and the moment when the last vehicle passes the same point plus, where appropriate, a time gap.

    Time Gap

    Within columns travelling under one movement number, a time gap is added to the calculated pass time. It represents the sum of the intervals between the columns and elements expressed in minutes.

    Traffic Density

    The average number of vehicles that occupy one kilometer of road space.
